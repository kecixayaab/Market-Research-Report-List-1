<p><p>The interbody cage market is segmented into metal and polymeric types. Metal interbody cages, typically made from titanium or stainless steel, offer high strength and biocompatibility, making them suitable for load-bearing applications. They are durable and provide a stable foundation for spinal fusion. In contrast, polymeric interbody cages, often made from PEEK or bioresorbable materials, are lighter, radiolucent, and can promote better bone integration. Each type serves specific clinical needs, influencing surgeon preference and patient outcomes in spinal surgeries.</p></p>
