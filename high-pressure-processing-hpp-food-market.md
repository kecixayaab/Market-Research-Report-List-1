<p><p>High Pressure Processing (HPP) food refers to a non-thermal method of preserving and sterilizing food products by applying high levels of hydraulic pressure, typically between 300 to 600 MPa. This technology effectively eliminates harmful pathogens and spoilage microorganisms while retaining the fresh taste, color, and nutritional value of the food. As a result, HPP is widely used in the production of juices, ready-to-eat meals, and meat products, enhancing food safety and shelf life without the need for preservatives.</p><p>The High Pressure Processing (HPP) Food Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by consumer demand for clean-label products and healthier food options.
