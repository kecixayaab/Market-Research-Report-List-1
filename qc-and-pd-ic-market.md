<p><ul><li>Downstream Facing Port (DFP)</li><li>Upstream Facing Port (UFP)</li><li>Dual Role Port (DRP)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The QC (Quick Charge) and PD (Power Delivery) IC market comprises three primary types of ports: Downstream Facing Port (DFP), Upstream Facing Port (UFP), and Dual Role Port (DRP). DFPs allow power and data to flow to downstream devices, typically acting as chargers. UFPs receive power and data from external sources, often found in devices like smartphones. DRPs can function as either DFPs or UFPs, providing versatility for interconnected devices to either supply or receive power as needed.</p></p>
