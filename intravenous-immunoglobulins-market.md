<p><p>The intravenous immunoglobulins (IVIG) market comprises several immunoglobulin types: IgG, IgA, IgM, IgE, and IgD. IgG constitutes the bulk of IVIG therapies, used for immune deficiencies and autoimmune disorders. IgA is beneficial for mucosal immunity, although less commonly used in IVIG formulations. IgM plays a role in primary immune responses, while IgE is primarily associated with allergic reactions and is not typically included in IVIG products. IgD has a minor role in serum but is crucial for B-cell function. Each type serves distinct clinical applications.</p></p>
