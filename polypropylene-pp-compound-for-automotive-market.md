<p><p>Polypropylene (PP) compounds used in the automotive market encompass several types. Mineral-filled PP enhances rigidity and reduces costs, while Thermoplastic Polyolefins (TPO) offer flexibility and durability for exterior applications. Thermoplastic Vulcanizates (TPV) combine rubber-like properties with thermoplastic behavior for enhanced performance. Glass-reinforced PP improves strength and thermal stability, making it suitable for structural components. Additive concentrates are used to enhance specific characteristics, such as UV resistance and processing aids. Additionally, other specialized formulations cater to niche applications within the automotive sector.</p></p>
