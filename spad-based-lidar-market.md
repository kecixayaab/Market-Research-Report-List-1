<p><ul><li>Solid State LiDAR</li><li>Mechanical LiDAR</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The SPAD-based LiDAR market is primarily divided into two categories: solid-state LiDAR and mechanical LiDAR. Solid-state LiDAR employs a fixed, non-moving design, utilizing Single Photon Avalanche Diodes (SPADs) for precise distance measurement, offering robustness and compactness, ideal for integration into various applications. In contrast, mechanical LiDAR uses rotating components to capture 360-degree scans, providing high-resolution 3D mapping, but often at the cost of increased size and complexity. Both types serve distinct market needs in autonomous vehicles, robotics, and geospatial applications.</p></p>
