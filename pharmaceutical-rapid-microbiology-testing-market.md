<p><ul><li>Immunology Based Methods</li><li>Nucleic Acid Based Amplification Tests</li><li>Mass Spectrometry Based Methods</li><li>Flow Cytometry Based Methods</li><li>Growth-Based Detection Technologies</li><li>Biochemical Tests</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The pharmaceutical rapid microbiology testing market comprises various methods. Immunology-based methods utilize antibodies for quick detection of pathogens. Nucleic acid-based amplification tests (NAATs) amplify specific DNA or RNA sequences for precise identification. Mass spectrometry-based methods analyze microbial mass spectra for rapid characterization. Flow cytometry-based methods assess cell properties, allowing for immediate quantification. Growth-based detection technologies focus on identifying microbial growth patterns, while biochemical tests assess metabolic characteristics, together enabling swift and reliable microbial detection in pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
