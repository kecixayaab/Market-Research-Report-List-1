<p><p>The pericardial patch market is segmented into three main types: autologous, xenograft, and synthetic. Autologous patches are derived from the patient's own tissue, minimizing rejection risks and promoting better integration. Xenograft patches are sourced from animal tissues, offering a readily available option with biocompatibility. Synthetic patches, made from materials like polymers, provide controlled properties and consistent quality. Each type has unique applications in cardiovascular surgeries, addressing specific clinical needs while considering patient safety and outcomes.</p></p>
